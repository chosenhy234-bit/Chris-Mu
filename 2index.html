About Me

My passion for economics began with something surprisingly ordinary: a meal at McDonald's. I noticed that the price of the familiar 12-yuan value meal gradually increased from 12 yuan to 12.9 yuan, and later to 13.9 yuan. As someone who frequently bought it because of its affordability, I naturally began asking why such a small change had happened. That simple observation opened the door to questions about production costs, supply and demand, pricing strategies, inflation, and consumer behavior.


Since then, I have enjoyed looking beyond everyday experiences to understand the economic logic that shapes them. Economics has taught me to think critically, analyze evidence, and approach problems with rationality instead of assumptions. What started as curiosity over the price of a meal has grown into a genuine academic passion and a desire to better understand how businesses, markets, and societies make decisions.

Activities & Background

As an international environmental volunteer in Bali, I participated in shoreline ecosystem restoration, marine plastic cleanup, and environmental education programs designed for local teenagers. Working alongside volunteers from different cultural backgrounds strengthened my teamwork, adaptability, and global perspective while reinforcing my commitment to sustainable development.


Beyond environmental service, I collaborated with charity fashion organizations to promote sustainable vintage fashion, encouraged plastic-free backstage practices during commercial events, and organized monthly community clothing donation campaigns to reduce textile waste. In parallel, I launched an independent market research project examining how different marketing strategies influence consumer behavior. By analyzing public reviews, comparing competing firms, and evaluating market performance, I developed a deeper understanding of strategic decision-making and evidence-based business analysis.
